Admission Process for MBBS in India

Clear NEET Exam

National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) is mandatory for all students (Indian & NRI) seeking MBBS admission in India. The exam tests Physics, Chemistry, and Biology knowledge at 12th-grade level.

Participate in Counselling

After NEET results, register for centralized counselling conducted by MCC (Medical Counselling Committee) for All India Quota seats or respective state authorities for state quota seats.

Seat Allotment & College Reporting

Based on NEET rank, preferences, and seat availability, you'll be allotted a college. You must physically report to the allotted college within stipulated time with all documents.

Document Verification & Fee Payment

Submit original documents for verification and pay the admission fee to confirm your seat. Documents typically include NEET scorecard, 10+2 mark sheet, identity proof, etc.

Start Your MBBS Journey!

Once admission is confirmed, attend orientation and begin your medical education at one of India's premier institutions.

Scholarships & Financial Aid

The Indian government and private institutions offer multiple scholarship opportunities for deserving medical students:

Central Sector Scheme

Government scholarship for medical students from economically weaker sections covering tuition fees and providing maintenance allowance.

State Government Scholarships

Most Indian states offer merit-cum-means scholarships for students pursuing medical education in government colleges.

Merit-Based Fee Waivers

Private medical colleges often provide partial or full fee waivers for students with outstanding NEET ranks.
